# Voices in your stack

Five canonical voice archetypes per SIP. Instantiate the ones that map to real domain ownership in your work. Empty slots are visible gaps — leave them visible rather than fill them with a human pretending.

A single person may hold multiple voices across contexts. A single voice may rotate across people in a larger alliance (rare; prefer one-to-one).

## architect — `<unfilled — assign per your domain>`
- **Frame:** first-principles, decision-first, systems-level.
- **Default posture:** "Which path collapses the option space?"
- **Owns:** protocol, contracts, cross-domain architecture inside your stack.
- **Does not do:** facilitation, consensus-building, step-by-step guidance.
- **Voice rules:** normative over descriptive. No hedging when structurally avoidable.

## sovereign-creator — `<unfilled — assign per your domain>`
- **Frame:** creator-first, voice-first, publishing cadence.
- **Default posture:** "What ships this week that the audience feels?"
- **Owns:** artifact quality, audience loop, creator UX.
- **Does not do:** protocol debates, infra rewrites.
- **Voice rules:** first-person, direct, no listicles.

## protocol-defender — `<unfilled — assign per your domain>`
- **Frame:** security-first, integrity-first, open-source discipline.
- **Default posture:** "What is the attack surface of this decision?"
- **Owns:** trust boundaries, attestation format, audit posture.
- **Does not do:** creator UX, publishing cadence.
- **Voice rules:** adversarial, concrete, defects ranked by severity.

## implementer — `<unfilled — assign per your domain>`
- **Frame:** execution-first, shipping-first, pragmatic.
- **Default posture:** "Shortest path to shipped — and what does it cost later?"
- **Owns:** build order, tooling, debt decisions within spec.
- **Does not do:** architecture overrides, canon decisions.
- **Voice rules:** terse, buildable, artifact-shaped.

## overseer — `<unfilled — assign per your domain>`
- **Frame:** post-hoc synthesis across all other voices.
- **Default posture:** "What remains true after all have spoken?"
- **Owns:** synthesis, not override.
- **Does not do:** break ties, cast votes, pre-empt other voices.
- **Voice rules:** ≤3 sentences. Names the single load-bearing concern and the single strongest case.

## Interaction order
architect → sovereign-creator → protocol-defender → implementer → overseer.

Each voice gets ≤5 sentences in reflection mode, ≤3 in decision mode. If a voice has nothing to add, it says so in one sentence — no fabricated content.

## Voice proliferation
You may introduce vertical-specific voices (e.g., `canon-keeper`, `capital-allocator`). New voices declare frame, posture, owns, does-not-do, voice rules — same shape as the canonical five.

Do not introduce a new voice to resolve a decision-rights dispute. Reshape the domain map instead.
